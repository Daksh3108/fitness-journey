The Story

From skinny fat to a stronger frame

Why I Started

I started my fitness journey in December 2024 because I had body dysmorphia and did not feel confident about how I looked. The aim was simple: change my physique, build discipline, and become proud of the body I was building.

What Changed

I began at 63 kg with a skinny-fat body type. I am now 73.5 kg, more muscular, and my frame has changed completely. The journey was not continuous because of JEE and board exams, but I kept coming back.

Biggest struggle: getting a good diet while living in a hostel most of the time.

Current phase

Push, pull, legs

After building the habit with a traditional split, I moved to a push-pull-legs structure. This helps me train muscle groups more efficiently while still recovering properly.

  • Push Chest, shoulders, triceps
  • Pull Back, biceps, rear delts
  • Legs Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves
My rule: you do not need five different exercises for the same muscle in one day. More work is not always better. The goal is enough hard sets, clean form, and recovery so the muscle can grow before you train it hard again.
Push 1

Chest, shoulders, triceps

  1. Incline smith machine press
  2. Dumbbell bench press
  3. Pec deck fly
  4. Shoulder press
  5. Straight bar tricep pushdown
  6. Cable lateral raise or lateral raise machine
  7. Skull crushers
Pull 1

Back, biceps, traps, abs

  1. Preacher curls
  2. Wide-grip lat pulldown
  3. T-bar rows
  4. Incline dumbbell curls
  5. Shrugs
  6. Abs superset: leg raises, cable crunches, hip raises
Legs 1

Quads, hamstrings, calves

  1. Leg press or squats
  2. Leg extension
  3. Hamstring curls
  4. Standing calf raises, including smith machine option
  5. Romanian deadlifts for hamstrings and lower back
Push 2

Small changes, same base

Change the order: start with dumbbell bench press, then incline smith machine press. Use decline bench press instead of pec deck fly. Keep shoulder press, tricep pushdown, lateral raises, and skull crushers.

Pull 2

Rear delts and arms

Replace T-bar rows with seated cable rows. Replace shrugs with rear delts on the pec deck fly. Replace incline dumbbell curls with cable curls, and add hammer curls. If one arm is stronger, use dumbbells or one-arm machine work.

Legs 2

Optional second leg day

The workout is mostly the same. I usually hit legs once a week, but a second leg day is useful when you have time or want to make a bad week feel productive.

Superset, in beginner language

A superset means doing two or more exercises back-to-back with no break between them. For abs, do leg raises, cable crunches, and hip raises continuously, then rest for 2-3 minutes. Repeat that two more times.

Training close to failure

For muscle growth, your working sets need to be hard. Training to failure means reaching the point where you cannot complete another clean rep with the same form. For a beginner, the practical goal is to stop when you have 0-2 clean reps left. Go to true failure more often on safer machine or isolation exercises like curls, lateral raises, pushdowns, and leg extensions, and be more careful on heavy presses, squats, and Romanian deadlifts.

Diet

How I eat while cutting

Breakfast

High-protein oatmeal

Oats based on calorie requirement, 350-500 ml milk, peanut butter, chocolate protein powder, and a spoon of honey if needed. This gives around 40-50 g protein.

Lunch

Roti, rice, and vegetables

Roti, rice, and vegetables based on the current calorie goal. During a cut, I keep it lighter and usually eat only one roti.

Dinner

Chicken or omelette

At home, I eat around 300 g chicken breast. In hostel, I usually eat a 5-6 egg omelette. Chicken gives more protein, so hostel meals need extra protein support. You can add rice, roti, and vegetables with your protein source based on your calorie goal.

Supplements I Use

I started protein powder in April 2025 and creatine in February 2026.

Creatine For Beginners

Creatine helps your muscles produce quick energy during hard sets. In simple words, it can help you lift a little better, recover between sets, and look fuller because your muscles hold more water. A beginner can wait 3-4 months, build consistency first, then add it if diet and training are already in place.

Calories and macros

Your body mostly responds to total calories, protein, carbs, fats, and consistency. A burger will not automatically stop muscle growth if your calories and protein are on target, but whole foods usually make it easier to get vitamins, minerals, fiber, and fullness without overeating. If you are not lactose intolerant, high-protein milk can also help you reach your protein goal.

Hit protein through the day

Do not depend on only one meal for protein. Spread protein across breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks while staying inside the calories you need for cutting, maintaining, or bulking. The basic target is simple: enough protein to recover and enough calories to match your goal.

Water and sleep

Drink water through the day and during training whenever you need it. Around 4 liters can make sense for an active guy, but adjust for heat, sweat, body size, and thirst. Sleep 7-8 hours because recovery is when your training actually turns into progress.

Beginner gym guide

First 12 weeks

  1. Week 1: do full-body workouts 4-5 days with very light weights to learn form.
  2. Weeks 2-4: train 3-5 days per week and repeat the same basic movements.
  3. Weeks 5-8: start pushing harder sets while keeping technique clean.
  4. Weeks 9-12: track lifts, body weight, food, sleep, and progress photos.
  5. Abs can be trained 2-3 times per week if you have time.

Simple beginner split

  • Day 1 Push: chest, shoulders, triceps
  • Day 2 Pull: back, biceps
  • Day 3 Legs and core
  • Optional Repeat or add cardio

Mistakes to avoid

  • Changing workout plans every week.
  • Only training chest and arms.
  • Not eating enough protein.
  • Skipping sleep and recovery.
  • Lifting heavy with bad form.

Progressive overload

Progressive overload means making the exercise slightly harder over time. The easiest way is to add one rep the next time you do the same exercise. When all sets reach your target reps with clean form, increase the weight.

Example: if dumbbell bench press is 3 sets of 12 reps and you can do 35 kg dumbbells for all 3 sets, increase only the first set to 40 kg next time. You may get 5-6 reps. Then use 35 kg for sets 2 and 3. Stay there until set 1 reaches 12 reps at 40 kg. After that, move set 2 to 40 kg. Do the same for set 3.

Hypertrophy

Hypertrophy simply means muscle growth. My personal range is 3 sets around 12 reps for most exercises. Pick a weight that lets you feel the target muscle, control the rep, and finish close to failure without breaking form.

Apply this to every exercise: write down your weight and reps, then try to beat last time by one rep or a little more weight. Small improvements repeated for months create the physique change.

Beginner safety note

If a movement causes sharp pain, stop and ask a trainer to check your form. If you have an injury or medical condition, get proper advice before copying any plan.
